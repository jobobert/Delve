# The Sixfold Realms — Lore & Reference

---

## The World

The Sixfold Realms are six distinct regions held in balance by an ancient mechanism called the **Sixfold Regulator** — a distributed device built by a civilization that is now memory and myth. Each region corresponds to one of the Regulator's six subsystems: **Flow**, **Heat**, **Pressure**, **Light**, **Growth**, and **Memory**. So long as the Regulator operates, the realms remain stable. The rivers run true. The mountain vents breathe in rhythm. The mist holds its pattern. The caverns' shadows do not devour.

The Regulator has been failing for a long time.

Nobody knows when it started, or whether it was sabotage, age, or accident. What they know is the effect: the Riverlands' currents echo voices that aren't there. The Dragon Peaks' vents pulse out of sequence. The Mistfen's paths fold back on themselves. Entropy spreads through the Gloamreach like a slow infection. The Mycelial Plains' spores dissolve memory. The Verdant Heartwood grows without direction, its ancient intelligence going to noise.

At the center of all six realms, beneath them all, is the **Core Chamber** — and inside it, the Regulator's heart. Its Overseer construct has sealed the chamber and gone into emergency lockdown, running an endless shutdown protocol that is slowly making things worse instead of better.

Five Regulator fragments have been pushed out into the regions by the failing system — one governs each subsystem except Flow, which remains anchored in the Riverlands. Recovering them and returning to the Core Chamber is the only way to stop the collapse.

| Realm | Governing Force | Crisis Symptom | Fragment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Riverlands Confluence | Flow | Echo phenomena; voices in the current | *(anchor zone — no fragment)* |

## Non-Hostile NPCs

### Riverlands Confluence

The Confluence is a working river town — a ferry hub where traders, crafters, guards, and travelers pass through. Most of its residents have noticed the strange echoes in the current but have no framework to understand what is happening. The town is the player's first home and most reliable base of operations.

---

**Ferryman Kale** (`kale_ferryman`) — *Ferrydock Square*
> "A weathered ferryman with river lines etched into his face."

Kale's eyes track the currents more than the people. He has been listening to the river longer than most, and the echoes have found him first. A quest giver and lore source, Kale is one of the few people who suspects the distortions are not natural. He accepts the **Resonant Token** as proof of the player's connection to the old rivers, rewarding trust and a small amount of gold.

---

**Mapmaker Sela** (`sela_mapmaker`) — *Ferrydock Square*
> "A sharp-eyed cartographer surrounded by half-finished maps."

Sela's frustration is professional: her maps keep going wrong. Routes she has walked a hundred times now end somewhere slightly different. She sells the **Resonant Token** and **Clarity Stew**, and her shop is a quiet acknowledgment that this problem has a market.

---

**Stallkeeper Brann** (`vendor_supplies`) — *Market Row*
> "A stocky merchant arranging goods with practiced efficiency."

Brann is the Confluence's most practical resident. He sells rope, torches, rations, minor medicines, and basic gear — everything a traveler needs to move through dangerous country. No words wasted, no opinions volunteered.

---

**Innkeeper Mara** (`innkeeper_mara`) — *Inn Common Room*
> "A broad-shouldered woman with tired eyes and a ready smile."

Mara has kept the inn running through floods, strange weather, and the current weirdness in the water. She offers rest (5 marks for full recovery) and rents **Guest Room Keys**. She is the Confluence's institutional memory — she knows who passed through, when, and why.

---

**River Child** (`river_child`) — *Mural Promenade*
> "A barefoot child tossing pebbles into the water."

No name given. No family visible. The River Child watches the ripples with unsettling focus, waiting for an answer. Their dialogue — *"Sometimes the river whispers names. Sometimes it forgets them"* — is the most direct statement in the Confluence about what the Regulator's failure feels like to those who live with it.

---

**Loran the Boatwright** (`crafter_boatwright`) — *Market Row*
> "A skilled craftsman who builds and repairs river vessels."

Decades on the water show in Loran's hands. He represents the quiet continuity of the Confluence — the work that keeps going regardless of what the river is doing. A crafter NPC available for commissions.

---

**Nessa the Realm-Walker** (`nessa_realm_walker`) — *Market Row*
> "A road-worn trader whose pack is covered in pins and tokens from distant places."

Nessa is the world's traveling merchant — her coat is stitched with patches from every zone. She sells cross-realm materials: the **Echo Pendant** (defensive neck piece), **Peak Forge Vial** (Dragon Peaks restoration), **Twilight Ink** (Gloamreach protection), **Bog Lens** (Mistfen headgear), **Spore Silk Sash** (Mycelial Plains carrying pack), and **Sap Salve** (Verdant Heartwood restoration). She is the only vendor who sells items from zones the player has not yet visited.

---

**Orin the Smith** (`rl_blacksmith_orin`) — *Blacksmith Shop*
> "A broad-shouldered blacksmith with iron-darkened hands and a direct manner."

Orin has been Confluence's working smith for twenty years. She shapes river-iron with the same quiet attention Loran gives timber. She sells the **River Iron Sword**, **Iron Pauldrons**, and repair kits. Her dialogue: *"River-iron holds an edge better than people expect. Come back when yours is dull."*

---

**Fenne the Root-Warden** (`rl_druid_fenne`) — *The Clearing*
> "A still figure in grey-green robes tending a small fire."

Fenne is a healer and teacher, camped at a clearing outside the town center. They offer rest at a reduced cost (3 marks) and teach the **Flowcurrent** fighting style to players who engage with their dialogue. Fenne can also spawn a **Sparring Partner** construct for combat practice. Their presence feels connected to the wider world — the herbs they tend carry the same resonance as the river.

---

**Old Aldric** (`rl_old_man_aldric`) — *Guest Room 2 (upstairs inn)*
> "A weathered old man sitting at the window, watching the river."

Aldric was a ferryman before his body made that impossible. He rents a room by the week and watches the water he used to work. He is the subject of the **Locket Return** quest — the player finds his **Locket** in the world and returns it, receiving his **River Scale Vest** in gratitude. His dialogue: *"I left a lot behind when the floodwaters came. Most of it didn't matter. One thing did."*

---

**Patrol Captain Veth** (`rl_patrol_captain`) — *City Center*
> "A lean woman in river-blue patrol gear reading the notice board."

Veth runs the Confluence patrol with minimal staff and considerable competence. She reads every notice herself. Her rules are clear: *"Keep your blade sheathed in the city center. That's not a request."* She represents the town's fragile order.

---

**Bank-Broker Sian** (`rl_banker`) — *Ferrydock Square*
> "A sharp-eyed broker behind a lacquered counter, quill ever-ready."

The Riverlands Confluence once had three competing banks. The floods took two. Sian runs what remains, serving merchants, ferryers, and adventurers alike. Standard banking services: deposit, withdraw, balance.

### Dragon Peaks

The Peaks are mountain country — a mining settlement carved into cliff faces, connected by rope bridges and wind-battered paths. The drake population is expanding its territory downslope, and the thermal vents that power the settlement's forges are cycling out of sync. The people here are practical, weather-hardened, and respect strength.

---

**Warden Selka Thornwright** (`warden_selka`) — *Mountain Watch Post*
> "A stern watchwoman wrapped in wind-leather."

Selka studies drake migration patterns and keeps the mountain watch. She believes dragons can be reasoned with, but not coddled. She is the keeper of the **Sky-Piercer** fighting style — available to players who prove themselves in the Peaks. She also functions as a quest giver for **Hoard of the Apex Drake**, accepting drakebone fragments as proof of a hunt. Combat note: if the player provokes her, her attacks intensify against those who bring discord — and soften for those with high prestige.

---

**Master Smith Orun Kett** (`smith_orun`) — *Highspire Main Street*
> "A broad-shouldered smith with soot-streaked hair."

Orun forges resonant alloys using ancient techniques from the Peaks' deep veins. He is the central figure of the **Forge the Sky-Piercer** quest — commissioning the legendary weapon requires his forge and his materials. He sells **Resonant Alloy Ingots**, **Volcanic Glass Shards**, and **Raw Ore**, and his dialogue encapsulates the Peaks' philosophy: *"Metal remembers the hands that shape it."*

---

**Tinker Apprentice** (`tinker_apprentice`) — *Tinkerer's Alcove*
> "A nervous youth tinkering with small gears."

Orun's apprentice fumbles with resonant coils in a cramped workshop. Their confidence is fragile, their hands are quick, and their main contribution to the player is color: *"Don't touch that — Orun will know if it's been moved."* They represent the generational transfer of craft knowledge that the Peaks depend on.

---

**Alloy Merchant Kar** (`vendor_alloys`) — *Alloy Market*
> "A trader with a satchel full of glowing ingots."

Kar buys and sells rare metals and watches miners with good luck very carefully. He sells **Resonant Alloy Ingots**, **Drakebone Fragments**, **Raw Ore**, and **Drake Scales** — all materials relevant to the zone's crafting quests. He knows the value of everything that comes out of the mountain.

---

**Miner Apprentice** (`miner_apprentice`) — *Resonant Mine Entrance*
> "A calloused miner with a hopeful grin."

Their pickaxe broke last week. They'll trade it for **Scrap Metal** — give them a piece and they hand back a patched **Rusty Pickaxe**. A small, self-contained exchange that rewards exploring the mine. *"Rich seam deeper in, if you've got the tools."*

### Mistfen Expanse

The Mistfen is a pressure-distorted swamp where paths fold back on themselves, distances stretch and compress, and the mist carries echoes of things that haven't happened yet. The Path-Readers have adapted — they listen to the swamp's rhythm rather than fighting it. Most travelers haven't.

---

**Path-Reader Sera** (`mf_path_reader_sera`) — *Path-Reader Encampment*
> "A calm figure with eyes half-closed, listening to the swamp."

Sera's posture is relaxed but precise — every breath measured against the Mistfen's pulse. She sells the tools needed to navigate the Expanse: **Anchor Stones** (which stabilize the mist loops), **Echo Compasses** (needed for the Drowned Vault), and **Anchor Pebbles**. She is the key figure of the **Anchors in the Mire** quest.

---

**Mistfen Smuggler** (`mf_swamp_smuggler`) — *Smuggler's Screen*
> "A hooded figure watching the paths with practiced wariness."

Mud-stained boots, quick eyes, and a habit of standing where the mist is thickest. The Smuggler lives in a clearing hidden from the Path-Readers' sight — which tells you everything. Non-hostile and non-talkative, their presence adds texture: the Mistfen is isolated enough that someone found it worth smuggling through.

---

### Gloamreach

The Gloamreach is a deep cavern system where light and shadow are active forces — tools and obstacles rather than just ambiance. Entropy is spreading upward from the core, detaching shadows from their sources and filling the deeper passages with constructs of unstable void-energy.

---

**Lightbearer Alen** (`gr_lightbearer_alen`) — *Twilight Mouth*
> "A weary traveler holding a lantern that never dims."

Alen has been at the cave mouth longer than he expected. His lantern maintains unnatural brightness, suppressing the entropy around him. He is a guide and equipment vendor — the **Bright Lantern**, **Dim Lantern**, **Shadow Crystal**, and supporting tools are all available here. A weary man doing a necessary job.

---

**Lightbearer Sira** (`gr_lightbearer_sira`) — *Twilight Mouth*
> "A calm figure surrounded by a faint halo of light."

Where Alen is worn down by the work, Sira is composed. Her presence steadies the air at the entrance, suppressing the cavern's entropy. She and Alen together form the Gloamreach's only reliable safe zone. Their paired presence suggests an order or tradition of Lightbearers whose purpose is exactly this.

---

**Echo-Touched Hermit** (`gr_echo_hermit`) — *Echo Warrens — Mirror Split*
> "A figure who flickers between moments."

The Hermit's form shifts subtly — caught between echoes of themselves. They live in the deeper warrens, past the shadow gates, in a chamber where reflections appear on the wrong wall. Their dialogue is cryptic navigation guidance for the Gloamreach's light-mechanic puzzles. Combat note: they are disoriented by bright light — if the player enters combat with a high-light source equipped, the Hermit's confusion spills outward as a status effect.

---

**Umbral Weaver** (`gr_umbral_weaver`) — *Echo Warrens — Shadowfall Pit*
> "A cloaked figure weaving strands of shadow-silk."

The Weaver works in a pit where shadows fall like water, stitching darkness into form with impossible precision. They are the Gloamreach's crafter — commissioning the **Umbral Mantle** (the zone's best armor) from gathered **Shadow Ore** and **Entropy Residue**. Their commission is the **gr_mantle** quest. *Hands that move with impossible precision, stitching darkness into form.*

---

### Mycelial Plains

Sporehaven was once a functioning settlement. The fungal network that underpins the Plains was always unusual — storing memories, modulating thought — but the Regulator kept it calibrated. Now the spores accumulate in the air, clouding cognition, dissolving short-term memory, and making the paths through the region increasingly difficult to navigate without preparation.

---

**Mayor Dellen Farrow** (`mp_mayor_dellen`) — *Loomroot Gate*
> "A kind but exhausted leader trying to keep Sporehaven functioning."

Dellen's eyes are tired and his thoughts wander mid-sentence. He fights creeping forgetfulness with stubborn determination. He is the town's civic anchor — still showing up, still trying. In combat (if provoked), he will comment if the player's own spore exposure is getting dangerous. His situation is a mirror of what happens to an entire community when memory starts to fail.

---

**Healer Rissa Vale** (`mp_healer_rissa`) — *Loomroot Gate*
> "A brewmaster who studies the fungus and its effects."

Rissa's **Clarity Brews** are what keep Sporehaven functioning. She speaks softly, as if afraid her own thoughts might slip away, but her formulations are precise. She is the zone's medical knowledge — understanding both the danger and the potential of what the Plains' fungus is doing.

---

**Memory-Touched Wanderer** (`mp_memory_wanderer`) — *Approach — Sporehaven Gate*
> "A person who drifts through the plains, half-lost in thought."

The Wanderer is the Mycelial Plains' most poignant figure. They move through the approaches speaking in fragments, hearing voices nobody else can hear. In combat (if spore exposure is high), they address the player directly: *"Do you hear them too? The memories… drifting…"* They are what happens to someone who stays in the Plains too long without protection.

---

**The Sporewarden** (`mp_sporewarden`) — *Fields — Loomroot Rise*
> "A cloaked figure who weaves fungal fibers into protective garments."

The Sporewarden's voice is calm and steady, untouched by the spores — an anomaly. They claim to remember the world before the malfunction, which makes them either very old or fundamentally different from the people around them. They are the zone's crafter, commissioning the **Sporewarden's Mantle** from gathered materials, and their presence at Loomroot Rise actually calms the ambient spore cloud.

---

### Verdant Heartwood

The Heartwood is a living forest whose roots network functions like a distributed intelligence. The Growth Regulator Fragment at its core has been broadcasting unchecked growth signals, turning the forest's natural defenses aggressive and making its internal logic increasingly inaccessible. The Root-Tenders are the people who have dedicated their lives to understanding and mediating with the Heartwood.

---

**Root-Tender Lyren** (`vh_root_tender_lyren`) — *Overgrown Canopy — Lower Path*
> "A calm figure woven with living bark patterns."

Lyren listens to the forest more than they speak. Their eyes reflect shifting green light. They are one of two Root-Tenders stationed at the Canopy entrance — the zone's first port of call, offering equipment (pollen masks, vine charms, resin sachets) and guidance for navigating the increasingly hostile forest paths.

---

**Root-Tender Syl** (`vh_root_tender_syl`) — *Overgrown Canopy — Lower Path*
> "A soft-spoken caretaker of the Heartwood's upper canopy."

Syl's hands glow faintly with restorative sap and they move with the rhythm of the trees. Where Lyren listens, Syl tends — maintaining the canopy, keeping the paths usable, trying to hold back the encroachment of hostile growth. Together with Lyren they represent the human element trying to maintain relationship with a forest that is no longer reliably communicating.

---

**Heartwood Shaper Thalen** (`vh_heartwood_shaper`) — *Heartwood Core — Growth Forge*
> "A master crafter who shapes living bark into armor."

Thalen's arms are wrapped in living wood. They work at the Growth Forge in the Heartwood Core, speaking with the authority of someone who has negotiated with the forest so long they've partly become it. They commission the **Heartwood Carapace** — the zone's finest armor — and are central to the **Carapace** quest. Their location deep in the Core means reaching them requires navigating most of the zone's puzzles.

---

### Core Chamber

The Core Chamber is not a natural place. It is entirely constructed — a descent through resonance-charged corridors, a lattice of calibration branches, and a final nexus guarded by the Overseer. The beings here are not people. They are remnants and functions of the Regulator itself.

---

**Calibration Sentinels** (`cc_light_sentinel`, `cc_pressure_sentinel`, `cc_growth_sentinel`, `cc_heat_sentinel`, `cc_memory_sentinel`) — *Calibration Branches*

Five non-hostile constructs, one per subsystem, each waiting in their respective calibration chamber. Each one is a projection of a region's governing force made solid — the Light Sentinel refracts into prismatic shards, the Growth Sentinel is woven roots and bark, the Pressure Sentinel stretches and compresses unnaturally. They will not attack. They are waiting for alignment. Calibrating with all five grants passage to the nexus and, ultimately, to the Crystal.

---

**The Central Crystal** (`cc_crystal`) — *Crystal Chamber*
> "The heart of the Sixfold Regulator."

It pulses weakly, cycling through five hues. It is not quite alive in any conventional sense — a pseudo-NPC that responds to dialogue, representing the Regulator's core intelligence. It offers the player a final choice: **restore** the Regulator to full function, or **disable** it permanently. Both are valid. Both have consequences.

## Significant Items

### Navigation & Puzzle Tools

**Anchor Stone** — The Mistfen Expanse's essential item. Attuned at the Anchor Field (requires 5 prestige), it stabilizes the mist loops that otherwise fold paths back on themselves. Without it, the looping mist ring cannot be exited westward, blocking access to the Echo Gate.

**Echo Compass / Echo Compass (Tuned)** — Purchased from Path-Reader Sera. The Resonance Pillar in the Anchor Field tunes it to the vault's frequency, setting the `mf_echo_gate_unlocked` flag and opening the locked entrance to the Drowned Vault.

**Bright Lantern / Shadow Crystal** — Gloamreach light tools with opposing functions. The Bright Lantern (`light_add = +2`) opens light-gated exits. The Shadow Crystal (`light_add = -1`) opens dim-gated exits that require low light. Both are required to navigate the full approach.

**Anchor Pebble** — Sold by Path-Reader Sera. A smaller attunement aid for fine-grained navigation in the Mistfen.

---

### Quest Items & Fragments

**Resonant Token** — A neck slot item (`+2 defense`) sold by Sela the Mapmaker. Giving it to Ferryman Kale triggers one of the Confluence's key lore moments: *"So the echoes have found you too."*

**RL Locket** — A quest item found in the world and returned to Old Aldric. Completing **Locket Return** earns the **River Scale Vest**.

**Thermal Conductor Piece** — Found in the Hearth of the Apex (Dragon Peaks). Picking it up completes step 3 of **Hoard of the Apex Drake** and grants 5 prestige. The item that proves the player has reached the dragon's inner sanctum.

**Drake Egg** — Also in the Hearth of the Apex. Picking it up (requires 40+ prestige) grants the **Drake Hatchling** companion. The egg represents what the Apex Drake was actually guarding.

**Drake Hoard Fragment** — Found on Hoard Ridge. Evidence of the Apex Drake's accumulated wealth; part of the outer quest chain.

**Pressure Regulator Fragment** — Core of the **Pressure Below** quest. Located in the Drowned Vault's Core after defeating the Vault Watcher.

**Shadow Regulator Fragment** — Core of the **Shadow Fragment** quest. Located in the Gloam Core's Fragment Chamber after defeating the Entropy Guardian. Uses the Echo Tuning Fork to access.

**Cognitive Regulator Fragment** — Core of the **Fragment** quest in the Mycelial Plains. Located in the Memory Garden's Fragment Chamber after defeating the Cognitive Core Guardian.

**Growth Regulator Fragment** — Core of the **Growth Regulator** quest in the Verdant Heartwood. Located in the Heartwood Core's Inner Sanctum after defeating the Core Guardian.

### Weapons

**Sky-Piercer Glaive** — The Dragon Peaks' signature weapon. `+12 attack, +3 defense`, 10% stun. Crafted via the **Forge the Sky-Piercer** quest through Smith Orun's commission: requires a **Resonant Alloy Ingot**, **Drakebone Fragment**, and **Volcanic Glass Shard**.

**Ashen Fang** (`dp_ashen_fang`) — Dragon Peaks weapon. Blade, `+10 attack`, **20% bleed** (magnitude 2). Crafted via the **The Ashen Fang** quest through Smith Orun's commission: requires **Volcanic Glass Shard**, **Drakebone Fragment**, and **Drake Feather Fragment**. Masterwork versions deal the highest bleed output of any weapon in the world. *"The blade burns faintly to the touch."*

**Pressure Chain** (`mf_pressure_chain`) — Mistfen Expanse weapon. Polearm, `+9 attack, +2 defense`, **10% stun**. Crafted via the **Weight of the Swamp** quest through Sera's commission: requires **Bog-Quartz** and **Pressure-Hollowed Stone**. The weapon stores the swamp's compressive force and releases it on impact.

**Entropy Edge** (`gr_entropy_edge`) — Gloamreach weapon. Blade, `+11 attack`, **18% bleed** (magnitude 2). Crafted via the **A Cut That Lasts** quest through the Umbral Weaver's commission: requires **Shadow Ore**, **Entropy Residue**, and a **Nullstone Shard**. The highest base attack of any craftable blade; the bleed reflects entropy seeping into wounds.

**Memory Whip** (`mp_memory_whip`) — Mycelial Plains weapon. Polearm, `+9 attack, +2 defense`, **12% stun**. Crafted via the **The Corrective Tool** quest through the Sporewarden's commission: requires **Fungal Fiber**, **Mycelial Resin**, and **Memory Spores**. The cognitive jolt on impact disrupts creature pattern-recognition mid-combat.

**Rootbound Stave** (`vh_rootbound_stave`) — Verdant Heartwood weapon. Polearm, `+10 attack, +3 defense`, **15% stun**. Crafted via the **The Living Weapon** quest through Thalen's commission: requires **Heartwood Bark**, **Sap Resin**, and **Growth Catalyst**. Still growing when completed — masterwork versions have formed into the shape of the owner's grip.

**Ferryman Hook** — Sold by Orin the Smith (Confluence). Polearm, `+5 attack`, 10% stun.

**River Iron Sword** — Sold by Orin the Smith. Blade, `+7 attack`.

**RL Resonant Blade** — Higher-tier blade, `+12 attack`. The Confluence's best sword.

### Armor

**River Scale Vest** — Quest reward from Old Aldric (Locket Return). Body slot, `+6 defense`. Has narrative weight — Aldric gives it freely in gratitude.

**Umbral Mantle** — Gloamreach commission reward. Body slot, `+3 defense, +5 max_hp`. Requires Shadow Ore and Entropy Residue.

**Sporewarden's Mantle** — Mycelial Plains commission reward. Body slot, `+2 defense, +8 max_hp`. The tanky option for the zone.

**Heartwood Carapace** — Verdant Heartwood commission reward. Body slot, `+8 defense, +10 max_hp`. The highest-defense craftable item in the world.

**CC Lattice Band** — Ring, dropped by the Overseer on death. `+3 defense, +10 max_hp`. The final boss reward.

### Tools & Consumables

**Rusty Pickaxe** — Required for mining ore nodes in the Dragon Peaks. Obtainable by trading Scrap Metal to the Miner Apprentice, or crafted via commission.

**Resonance Forge Panel** — Not a carried item but an interactive scenery object in the Hearthforge Ruin. Activating it aligns the vents and unlocks the thermal gate, progressing the Drake Hoard quest.

**Clarity Brew** — Reduces spore exposure by 20. Essential for navigating the Mycelial Plains' gated paths and the Memory Garden.

**Fungal Mask** — Head slot armor (`+1 defense`) that halves spore gain per room. The most efficient long-term protection in the Plains.

**Resonance Shard** — Dropped by Core Chamber enemies. Crafting material for the final zone.

**Harmonic Core** — Dropped by Resonance Sentinels. Rare crafting material.

## Significant Rooms

### Riverlands Confluence

**Ferrydock Square** (`rl_ferrydock_square`) — The starting room and hub of the Confluence. Kale the Ferryman, Sela the Mapmaker, and the Banker are all here. The square functions as the world's main trading post and exposition point for the overall crisis.

**The Echoing Chamber** (`rl_echoing_chamber`) — End point of the **Rivers Remember** quest. The chamber answers the current's echoes. Entering it with the quest active and the Resonant Token in hand completes the quest and grants prestige.

**The Clearing** (`rl_clearing`) — Fenne the Root-Warden's camp. The only place in the Confluence where a fighting style can be learned and a sparring partner can be spawned. Quiet, slightly outside the town — a liminal space.

**Rl Ferry Berth — Peaks** (`rl_ferry_berth_peaks`) — The inter-zone crossing point. The world's physical connection between the Confluence and Dragon Peaks.

---

### Dragon Peaks

**Highspire Main Street** (`dp_highspire_1`) — The Peaks' hub. Smith Orun is stationed here. Entering triggers the first step of **Hoard of the Apex Drake** and the zone's introductory message. The start room for the zone.

**Resonant Mine Entrance** (`dp_mine_entrance`) — The notice board reads: *"Pickaxe required. Watch for cave rats."* The mine holds the Raw Ore nodes needed for the Sky-Piercer commission.

**Hearthforge Ruin** (`dp_emberfall_5`) — An ancient forge buried in ash with a still-functional resonance panel. Activating it aligns the Emberfall vents and unseals the Thermal Conduit Gate — the gateway to the Apex Domain.

**Thermal Conduit Gate** (`dp_apex_approach_3`) — The locked threshold between the mountain proper and the dragon's territory. Opened by the forge alignment. Guarded by an Apex Drake Guard.

**Hearth of the Apex** (`dp_apex_inner_2`) — Contains the **Thermal Conductor Piece** and the **Drake Egg**. The penultimate room before the boss — lore-rich, with both the quest objective and the companion hook.

**Apex Summit** (`dp_apex_inner_3`) — Boss room. The Apex Drake. The highest point in the world. *"Its shadow swallowing the light, then lands with the certainty of something that has never lost."*

---

### Mistfen Expanse

**Path-Reader Encampment** (`mf_path_reader_camp`) — The Mistfen's anchor point. No enemies, town-flagged, Sera's base. The zone's start room.

**Anchor Field** (`mf_anchor_field`) — Where Anchor Stones are attuned and the **Anchors in the Mire** quest advances. The Resonance Pillar here tunes the Echo Compass. Three key items spawn here as interactable nodes.

**The Mist Loop** (`mf_mist_loop1` / `mf_mist_loop2` / `mf_mist_loop3`) — A three-room loop that without an attuned Anchor Stone routes endlessly east → east → east → back to loop 1. Solving it (carrying the Anchor Stone) completes the first quest and opens the western path toward the Echo Gate. Each segment carries the echo wraith.

**Echo Gate** (`mf_echo_gate`) — A stone arch requiring a tuned Echo Compass in the recess. Serves as the locked gate to the Drowned Vault.

**Drowned Vault — Core** (`mf_vault_core`) — End point. The Pressure Regulator Fragment floats above the water. Past the Vault Watcher. *"A crystalline device floats above the water: the Pressure Regulator fragment, pulsing with uneven force."*

---

### Gloamreach

**Twilight Mouth** (`gr_entrance`) — The only town-flagged room in the Gloamreach. Both Lightbearers and the Banker are here. The zone's light-mechanic tools are all available before entering the cavern proper.

**Echo Warrens — Mirror Split** (`gr_warrens4`) — Where the Echo-Touched Hermit lives. Past the shadow gate, requiring the Shadow Crystal to enter. The zone's navigational puzzle pays off here.

**Echo Warrens — Shadowfall Pit** (`gr_warrens6`) — The Umbral Weaver's workshop. Shadow Ore nodes and the commission NPC in the same room. *"A pit where shadows fall like water."*

**Gloam Core — Entropy Heart** (`gr_core2`) — Boss room. The Entropy Guardian. A pulsing mass of shadow-crystal. The fragment is one room beyond.

**Gloam Core — Fragment Chamber** (`gr_core3`) — *"A shard of the Regulator floats in a column of dim light."* The Shadow Regulator Fragment. The quest completes on entering after the guardian is down.

---

### Mycelial Plains

**Loomroot Gate** (`mp_entrance`) — Town hub. Mayor Dellen, Healer Rissa, and the Banker. The shop here sells the zone's essential navigation tools: Clarity Brew and Fungal Mask.

**Approach — Fresh Air Pocket** (`mp_approach4`) — One of the few rooms that actively reduces spore exposure (-10) and sets the `mp_mind_clear` flag. Players who find it early gain a significant advantage in navigating the gated paths.

**Fields — Loomroot Rise** (`mp_fields5`) — The Sporewarden's hill. Their presence alone reduces ambient spore exposure (-5) and sets `mp_mind_clear`. A peaceful safe zone in the middle of hostile territory.

**Memory Garden — Gate** (`mp_garden_entrance`) — Enforces the zone's cognitive threshold: if spore exposure is above 30, the archway teleposts the player back. The garden itself requires a clear mind to enter. *"The archway hums approvingly as your clear mind crosses the threshold."*

**Memory Garden — Cognitive Core** (`mp_garden5`) — Boss room. The Cognitive Core Guardian. *"A massive fungal heart pulses with ancient knowledge."*

### Verdant Heartwood

**Heartwood Threshold** (`vh_edge`) — The entry point, where the Riverlands path ends at a wall of living roots. Carrying the **Bog-Quartz Heart** from the Mistfen causes the roots to recognize the player.

**Overgrown Canopy — Listening Grove** (`vh_canopy2`) — A circle of trees whose roots pulse faintly. Carrying the **Memory Root Cluster** later causes them to hum in recognition. One of the most purely atmospheric rooms in the world.

**Rootbound Warrens — Illusion Fork** (`vh_warrens4`) — Three paths appear; only the eastern one (gated by the `vh_true_path` flag set by finding the Memory Root Cluster) is real. The other two instantly teleport the player back. Combat and puzzle in one room.

**Heartwood Core — Growth Forge** (`vh_core2`) — Heartwood Shaper Thalen's forge. The crafting station for the Carapace commission. *"A living forge of roots and sap. It responds to your presence."*

**Heartwood Core — Inner Sanctum** (`vh_core4`) — Boss room. The Core Guardian. The Growth Regulator Fragment floats within the root-heart.

### Core Chamber

**Chamber Threshold** (`cc_entrance`) — Resets resonance to 100 on every entry. Town-flagged; the Banker is here. Starts step 1 of the **Finale** quest. The only rest point in the Core Chamber.

**Descent — Calibration Gate** (`cc_descent7`) — The threshold between descent and the Lattice. Requires resonance ≥ 60 to pass. The five pedestals bearing regional symbols make it explicit: this gate knows where you have been.

**Lattice — Convergence Hub** (`cc_lattice1`) — The five-branched intersection at the center of the Core. One path per subsystem. Passage down to the Nexus requires calibrating all five — meaning the player must enter each calibration branch, defeat its Echo, and align its Sentinel.

**Calibration Rooms** (`cc_light_calibration`, `cc_pressure_calibration`, `cc_growth_calibration`, `cc_heat_calibration`, `cc_memory_calibration`) — Each room mirrors one of the five outer realms in its appearance: floating crystals and mirrors for Light, air-bending geometry for Pressure, living vines for Growth, thermal vents for Heat, whispering fungal nodes for Memory. In each, the player faces an Echo (hostile, no respawn) and then talks to the Sentinel (friendly) to align the subsystem.

**Overseer's Arena** (`cc_overseer_arena`) — The penultimate room. The Overseer's presence has been felt throughout the descent. Here it takes form. Combat note: resonance affects the fight directly — low resonance triggers bonus damage from the Overseer; high resonance triggers a protective shimmer at round 5+.

**Crystal Chamber** (`cc_crystal_chamber`) — The Regulator's heart. The Central Crystal offers the final choice: **restore** or **disable**. Both grant prestige. The world ends quietly, one way or another.
