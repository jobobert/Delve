# The World of the Sixfold Realms

**A Narrative Backstory**

Long before the current settlements rose along the rivers, the world was a wild and unstable place. Storms raged without pattern, forests grew and withered in the span of days, and the land itself shifted like a restless sleeper. The ancient architects — an enigmatic civilization whose name has been lost to time — sought to tame this chaos. They terraformed the world, spliting it into many sections. Each section is controlled by an environmental regulator that calms and coaleses the chaos into a habital world. Each regulator lives at the center of its section, tts purpose simple and monumental: to harmonize the world’s climates, ecosystems, and elemental forces.

Each regulator is comprised of multiple independant yet connected pieces, each attuned to a different environmental domain: tempurature, pressure, flora and fauna, even light and psyche.

For centuries, the device worked flawlessly. Seasons stabilized. Ecosystems flourished. Civilizations rose. But the architects vanished, leaving behind only their creation — and the warning etched into the Core Chamber walls: “Balance is not a state. It is a choice renewed.”

When the device in our section failed it did so catastrophically.Each piece of the regulator scattering across the section. Immediately the central control system erected an impassible wall to isolate this section from the rest of the world. Engineers were dispached to collect the pieces, but the system's design required a 500 year reset cycle before repairs could be made. The engineers steeled for the long wait, but as time went on factions grew within the isolated population. The engineers, sensing the coming violence, hid their pieces across the section and provided instructions for the eventual use. And when the fighting came the engineers each lost their lives but never revealed their secrets.

As the reset period expired the pieces, still separated began to operate in unexpected ways. No one noticed the changing environment at first. A strange wind here, a sudden bloom there. But soon, each region began to twist into an exaggerated version of itself. The Peaks grew volatile. The Mistfen looped and distorted. The Heartwood overgrew. Gloamreach dimmed. The Plains whispered and forgot. And the Riverlands — once the world’s calm center — became a crossroads of instability, its currents carrying echoes of every region’s unraveling.

Now, the device lies fractured. Its six pieces scattered. Its systems misaligned. And the section teeters on the edge of environmental collapse. Only those who can navigate the realms, earn the trust of their people, and restore the ancient machine stand a chance of saving the section from returning to the chaos that existed before the architects’ intervention.

## The Story Arc
**A full narrative progression from Level 1 to the Core Chamber finale**

### ACT I — The Rivers Remember

> Starting Region: Riverlands Confluence

The story begins in the Riverlands, where ferrymen whisper of strange currents and druids sense a growing imbalance. The player learns that each region is suffering from environmental distortions—and that the Riverlands, once the world’s stabilizing heart, is now absorbing the chaos from all sides.

Early quests introduce the idea that the world is interconnected. The rivers carry not just water, but echoes of the failing device. NPCs like Ferryman Kale, Mapmaker Sela, and Druid Theren hint that something ancient beneath the world is stirring.

The player’s first major revelation comes when they discover a fragment of an ancient inscription: “Five conduits feed the heart. When one falters, all tremble.”

This sets the stage for the journey outward.

---

### ACT II — The Fractured Realms

> Regions 1–5: The Outer Realms

Each region represents a different environmental failure:

**Dragon Peaks — The Winds Unbound**

Volatile updrafts, unstable heat vents, and aggressive drakes signal that the atmospheric regulator is failing. The player retrieves the Thermal Conductor, the first device piece.

**Mistfen Expanse — The Paths That Loop**

Spatial distortions and looping terrain reveal a malfunction in the humidity and pressure systems. The player stabilizes the Mistfen Heart and recovers the Pressure Regulator.

**Verdant Heartwood — Growth Without Restraint**

The forest grows faster than it can sustain itself. Living tools rebel. The player calms the Heartwood Pulse and retrieves the Growth Harmonizer.

**Gloamreach — The Light That Fades**

Twilight deepens into near darkness. Shadows behave with intent. The player crafts the Lens of Truth and recovers the Light Modulator.

**Mycelial Plains — Memory in the Spores**

The fungal network becomes sentient, whispering and forgetting in equal measure. The player maps the psychic network and retrieves the Cognitive Regulator.

Each region’s story reinforces the same truth: The world is not dying—it is reverting. Without the device, nature returns to its primordial chaos.

### ACT III — The Descent

> Region 6: The Core Chamber

With all five outer device pieces recovered, the player descends into the Core Chamber. Here, they discover the truth: the device is not merely malfunctioning—it is rejecting its own programming. The ancient architects designed it to require periodic recalibration, but with their disappearance, the system has been running unchecked for centuries.

The Core Overseer, a construct of pure resonance, has been trying to shut the device down to prevent catastrophic overload. But without guidance, its attempts have destabilized the entire world.

The player must:
- Recalibrate the subsystems
- Realign the central crystal
- Install all six device pieces
- Defeat the Overseer’s final manifestation
- Restore the Sixfold Regulator

As the device stabilizes, the world begins to breathe again. Winds calm. Mists settle. Forests slow their growth. Shadows retreat. Spores quiet. Rivers return to their steady pulse.

The final inscription appears: “Balance restored is balance earned.”

### ACT IV — Epilogue: The Choice Renewed

The world is saved—but the device is ancient, and its future depends on those who understand it. NPCs across the regions react to the restored balance, and the player is left with a lingering question:

Should the world remain regulated… or should nature reclaim its freedom?

This sets up:
- Future expansions
- Moral choices
- Faction conflicts
- Post game world states

#### Spore Exposure (Player Attribute)

- `spore_exposure` tracked as a world-defined player attribute (`[[player_attrs]]` in config.toml, min=0 max=100 default=0, display=bar)
- Increases via `adjust_attr name="spore_exposure" amount=N` in room on_enter scripts (dense bloom rooms: +3/+4; fields: +1/+2)
- Decreased by clarity brews (`adjust_attr amount=-15`), Healer Rissa's tonic, or clarity grove rooms
- Wearing `mp_fungal_mask` halves spore gain (if_item check in on_enter)
- Memory Garden entry gated at spore_exposure < 30

#### mp_mind_clear Flag (Exit Gating)

- Clarity rooms (`set_flag mp_mind_clear`) and bloom rooms (`clear_flag mp_mind_clear`) toggle this flag
- Spore-sensitive exits use `show_if = { op = "has_flag", flag = "mp_mind_clear" }` — this is the only way to gate exits on attr values (show_if does not support attr conditions directly)

#### Sporewarden Mantle (Commission-Based)

- The mantle is crafted via the `commission` command with mp_sporewarden (not dialogue inline)
- `crafting/mp_sporewarden.toml` defines two commissions: `mp_weave_mantle` (mantle from fungal_fiber + clarity_cap, 12 turns) and `mp_brew_clarity` (clarity_brew × 2 from memory_spores + mycelial_resin)
- mp_sporewarden dialogue directs players to use `commission` command rather than offering inline crafting

#### cc_finale Quest Trigger

- The finale quest (`cc_finale`) is **started by Mayor Dellen** when the player says "I have recovered all five fragments" — he grants the `start_quest cc_finale` op and directions to the Core Chamber descent

#### Status Effects

- confused (used in Mistfen, Plains, Core Chamber)
- spores (applied by fungal enemies)

>Cross Region Tie: Loomroot trades with Region 4 for reflective cloaks that help slow fungal growth in shaded areas.

The chamber is not magical — it’s ancient technology, humming with low resonance, powered by geothermal vents and crystal conduits.

### Resonance Player Attribute

- `resonance` is tracked as a world-defined player attribute (`[[player_attrs]]` in config.toml, min=0 max=100 default=100, display=bar)
- **Resets to 100** when entering `cc_entrance` (`set_attr name=”resonance” value=100` in on_enter) — prevents carry-over between visits
- Drains per descent room via `adjust_attr name=”resonance” amount=N` (−2 to −4 per room)
- **Restored +15** per sentinel calibration dialogue (`adjust_attr name=”resonance” amount=15`)
- cc_descent7 exit gated: on_enter sets `cc_gate_stable` flag only when resonance ≥ 60; show_if uses `has_flag cc_gate_stable`
- Overseer round_script deals bonus damage at resonance ≤ 40; grants one protected turn at resonance ≥ 80 after round 5

### 🏛️ Structure of the Core Chamber

The chamber follows a linear descent with calibration side rooms.

1. **Entrance & Descent** (`cc_entrance` → `cc_descent1`–`cc_descent7`)

    Player enters, resonance resets to 100. Descent rooms drain resonance steadily. Echo NPCs spawn as enemies. A calibration gate at cc_descent7 blocks progress if resonance < 60 (flag-based show_if).

2. **Calibration Wing** (`cc_heat_room`, `cc_growth_room`, `cc_light_room`, `cc_pressure_room`, `cc_memory_room`)

    Five rooms, each with a non-hostile sentinel NPC whose dialogue restores +15 resonance and sets the corresponding alignment flag (`cc_heat_aligned`, etc.). Each room also spawns one echo enemy. Calibration is done entirely via NPC dialogue — **not room on_use puzzles**.

3. **Convergence Nexus** (`cc_nexus_entrance`, `cc_nexus_hub`, `cc_nexus_hall`, `cc_lattice1`)

    Entry to cc_nexus_entrance checks all five alignment flags via sequential `if_not_flag` + `teleport_player` guards; sets `cc_all_calibrated` and advances `cc_finale` step 2. The down exit at cc_lattice1 requires `cc_all_calibrated` (show_if).

4. **Overseer Arena** (`cc_overseer_arena`)

    Static spawn of `cc_overseer_final`. The east exit (to crystal chamber) requires `cc_overseer_defeated` (show_if). Kill script advances quest step 3 and gives `cc_lattice_band` (boss drop ring).

5. **Crystal Chamber** (`cc_crystal_chamber`)

    `cc_crystal` NPC offers two choices: **Restore** or **Disable** the Sixfold Regulator. Both paths complete `cc_finale`, grant 5 prestige, and set the corresponding outcome flag (`cc_reboot_restore` or `cc_reboot_disable`).

## Two Endings

### 🌟 ENDING 1 — Restore the Regulator

**Theme:** Hope through protection.

Outcome
- The device reboots; resonance restored to all regions
- Sets `cc_reboot_restore` flag
- Player gains 5 prestige; finale quest complete
- World breathes again — winds calm, mists settle, forests slow

### 🔥 ENDING 2 — Disable the Regulator

**Theme:** Hope through freedom.

Outcome
- Shutdown sequence initiated; resonance dissipates
- Sets `cc_reboot_disable` flag
- Player gains 5 prestige; finale quest complete
- Nature reclaims its freedom

## 🧭 NPC Reactions Based on Prestige

High Prestige
- NPCs trust your decision
- Settlements celebrate your leadership
- You receive honorary titles or roles

Neutral Prestige
- NPCs debate your choice
- Some support you, others question you
- The world feels complex and alive

Low Prestige
- NPCs fear or resent your decision
- Some settlements may distance themselves
- You gain a reputation as a dangerous visionary

## 🌿 Unified Quality‑Tier Standard (applies to all commissions)
All commissions now use the same three‑tier structure:

### Tier 1 — Unsteady / Crude / Raw
- Quality: 0–39
- Represents rushed work, low skill, or disharmony.
- Slight penalties or reduced stats.

### Tier 2 — Standard / Harmonized / Tempered
- Quality: 40–79
- Baseline expected result.
- No bonuses or penalties.

### Tier 3 — Exceptional / Perfect / Masterwork
- Quality: 80–100
- Represents mastery, high prestige, or special flags.
- Grants small bonuses or a thematic proc.
